The Program
The boardroom is the last line of defense.
Make it the strongest.

In an era of unprecedented financial complexity — from volatile markets and cyber risk to ESG mandates and AI-driven disruption — boards of directors can no longer delegate risk oversight to committees alone. Risk Governance Mastery is the definitive executive program designed to equip board members and C-suite leaders with the strategic frameworks, regulatory fluency, and decision-making tools required to govern risk at the highest level.

Led by Moisés Tiktin, former CEO of Banco Monex & Monex Casa de Bolsa and one of Mexico's most respected financial leaders, this program distills four decades of boardroom experience into an intensive, practitioner-led executive program. Participants will master enterprise risk architecture, regulatory compliance across jurisdictions, crisis governance protocols, and the emerging risk landscape shaped by AI, climate, and geopolitical volatility.

This is not a theoretical course. Every framework has been battle-tested in real boardrooms, real crises, and real regulatory environments across Latin America and global markets.

Curriculum
Cinco sesiones presenciales.
A complete risk governance transformation.
01
3 Hours

Foundations of Risk Governance

The evolving role of the board in risk oversight. Governance vs. management: drawing the line. International frameworks (COSO ERM, ISO 31000) and their practical application for directors. Building a risk-aware board culture from the top.

02
3 Hours

Enterprise Risk Architecture

Designing the enterprise risk management framework. Risk appetite statements, tolerance thresholds, and cascading metrics. Integrating risk into strategic planning and capital allocation decisions at the board level.

03
3 Hours

Market, Credit & Liquidity Risk for the Boardroom

Understanding financial risk without being a quant. Key metrics boards must monitor: VaR, Expected Shortfall, credit exposure, liquidity coverage ratios. Translating risk reports into strategic decisions. Real case studies from Mexican and global financial institutions.

04
3 Hours

Regulatory Compliance & Cross-Border Risk

Navigating the regulatory landscape: CNBV, Banxico, Basel IV, Dodd-Frank, MiFID II. Cross-border risk governance for multinational operations. Anti-money laundering, sanctions compliance, and the board's fiduciary duty in regulatory matters.

05
3 Hours

Operational, Cyber & Technology Risk

The board's role in operational resilience. Cybersecurity governance frameworks (NIST CSF, ISO 27001). Third-party and supply chain risk oversight. Building a technology risk committee that actually works.

06
3 Hours

ESG, Climate & Reputational Risk

ESG risk as a board-level strategic priority. Climate risk disclosure (TCFD/ISSB) and scenario analysis. Reputational risk governance in the age of social media and stakeholder activism. Integrating ESG into the risk appetite framework.

07
3 Hours

Crisis Governance & Stress Testing

Board-level crisis management protocols. Designing and interpreting stress tests and scenario analyses. Lessons from real financial crises: what boards did right and wrong. Communication governance during crisis events.

08
3 Hours

Capstone: Your Risk Governance Blueprint

Integrate all seven modules into a comprehensive, board-ready risk governance blueprint for your organization. Peer review and faculty feedback. Leave with an actionable plan to elevate risk oversight in your boardroom.

Moisés Tiktin Nickin

Former CEO, Banco Monex & Monex Casa de Bolsa · Independent Board Director · ITAM Faculty

Moisés Tiktin es uno de los ejecutivos más experimentados del sector financiero mexicano, con más de cuatro décadas en banca, mercados de capitales, gestión de riesgos y gobierno corporativo. Economista por el ITAM (con honores), con estudios de posgrado y doctorado en Finanzas y Economía Internacional en la Universidad de Chicago; en 2025 obtuvo la certificación Certified Chair Executive Program del Advisory Board Centre.

Su carrera inició en Valores Finamex Casa de Bolsa — donde ascendió hasta CEO — y cofundó Valorum Operadora de Sociedades de Inversión. En 2001 se incorporó a Grupo Financiero Monex: CEO de Monex Casa de Bolsa durante 10 años y CEO de Banco Monex durante 11, supervisando las subsidiarias del grupo en Londres, Ámsterdam, Madrid, Toronto, Singapur, Washington D.C., Nueva York y Los Ángeles.

Hoy es Consejero Independiente: Monex Grupo Financiero (Consejo, Comité de Riesgos y de Crédito), Vitalis (Afore), Hospital ABC (Comité de Finanzas), Carbon Plus y el Advisory Board del ITAM — con participación histórica en los consejos de la ABM, la Bolsa Mexicana de Valores, la Contraparte Central de Valores y la AMIB. Es Profesor de Economía y Finanzas en el ITAM y columnista de El Economista. La perspectiva rara de quien gestionó el riesgo como CEO y lo supervisó como consejero.
